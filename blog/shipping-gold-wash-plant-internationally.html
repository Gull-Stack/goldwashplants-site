CIF vs FOB: Understanding Your Shipping Terms

Most of our international customers choose between two shipping arrangements:

FOB (Free on Board) - Port of Los Angeles/Long Beach:

  • We deliver to the port and load onto your shipping vessel
  • You arrange and pay for ocean freight and destination handling
  • You handle customs clearance at destination port
  • Best if you have established freight forwarders
  • Typically saves 15-25% on total landed cost

CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight) - Your Destination Port:

  • We arrange complete door-to-port delivery
  • Includes ocean freight, marine insurance, and export documentation
  • You still handle customs clearance and import duties
  • Simplest option for first-time international buyers
  • We can quote CIF to most major ports worldwide

Telex Release and Documentation

For smooth customs clearance, ensure these documents are ready:

  • Commercial Invoice: Detailed equipment description and values
  • Packing List: Complete parts inventory for each container
  • Bill of Lading: Original or telex release (faster)
  • Certificate of Origin: Required for duty calculations
  • Technical Specifications: May be required by some countries

Telex Release Advantage: Instead of waiting for original documents to arrive by courier, telex release allows immediate customs processing with electronic documents. This saves 3-5 days and reduces demurrage charges.

Customs Brokers: Your Essential Partner

A good customs broker makes the difference between smooth delivery and weeks of delays. Choose brokers who:

  • Have specific experience with mining equipment
  • Understand HS codes for wash plants (usually 8474.10 or 8474.90)
  • Can handle temporary import permits if you're testing equipment
  • Offer port storage if customs clearance takes time
  • Provide accurate duty and fee calculations upfront

Timeline Planning: What to Expect

Here's the realistic timeline for international wash plant delivery:

  1. Manufacturing: 4-8 weeks depending on model and options
  2. Pre-shipment preparation: 1 week for documentation and container loading
  3. Ocean transit: 2-4 weeks depending on destination
  4. Customs clearance: 3-10 days (varies greatly by country)
  5. Inland transport: 1-5 days depending on distance from port

Total timeline: Plan for 8-15 weeks from order to delivery at your site.

Cost Factors and Hidden Expenses

Beyond the equipment cost, budget for these shipping-related expenses:

  • Ocean freight: $3,000-$15,000 depending on destination and size
  • Import duties: 0-25% of equipment value (varies by country)
  • Port handling: $500-$2,000 per container
  • Customs broker fees: $800-$1,500 for complete clearance
  • Inland transport: $1,000-$5,000+ depending on distance
  • Storage fees: $50-$150 per day if clearance is delayed

Regional Considerations

Africa: Plan extra time for customs. Port infrastructure varies widely. Consider regional hubs like Durban for southern Africa distribution.

South America: Peru and Chile have excellent mining equipment import processes. Venezuela and Bolivia have more complex procedures.

Pacific Islands: Limited shipping schedules require precise timing. Consider air freight for critical spare parts.

Europe: Excellent logistics but high costs. Consider used equipment options for cost savings.

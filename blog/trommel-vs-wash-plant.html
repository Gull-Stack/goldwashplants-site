Understanding the Difference

"Should I buy a trommel or a wash plant?" It's one of the most common questions in placer mining. The answer depends on your operation, but let's break down the key differences.

What Is a Gold Trommel?

A gold trommel is a rotating cylindrical screen that classifies material by size. Raw material enters one end, and as the drum rotates, smaller particles fall through the screen holes while oversized material exits the other end. Some trommels include integrated sluice boxes for recovery, while others are purely classification devices.

What Is a Gold Wash Plant?

A gold wash plant is an all-in-one system that combines a trommel (or vibrating screen) with a sluice box recovery system. It handles the entire process: feeding, scrubbing, classifying, and recovering gold. No additional equipment needed.

When to Choose a Trommel

  • You already have a sluice box or recovery system
  • You only need classification, not recovery
  • Budget is the primary concern
  • You're processing material that doesn't need aggressive scrubbing

When to Choose a Wash Plant

  • You want an all-in-one solution with no additional equipment
  • You need maximum gold recovery (95%+)
  • Simplicity and fast setup are priorities
  • You're running a commercial operation where efficiency matters

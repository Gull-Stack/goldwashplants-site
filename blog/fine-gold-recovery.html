What Is Fine Gold?

Fine gold generally refers to gold particles smaller than 30 mesh (0.6mm). "Flour gold" is even smaller â€” 100 mesh (0.15mm) and below. At these sizes, gold particles behave more like sand than nuggets, and standard riffles can't trap them effectively.

Why Standard Equipment Fails

Most wash plants and sluice boxes are designed for coarse gold. Their riffle patterns create turbulence zones optimized for larger particles. Fine gold particles get swept right over standard riffles and out the end of the sluice. This is where money is literally flowing away.

Tips for Maximizing Fine Gold Recovery

  • Proper classification: Remove oversized material before it reaches your sluice. Our integrated trommel handles this automatically.
  • Correct water flow: Too much water washes fine gold away. Too little causes packing. Our systems are calibrated for optimal flow.
  • Clay breaking: Fine gold trapped in clay won't be captured unless the clay is broken apart first. Our aggressive scrubbing action frees clay-bound gold.
  • Regular cleanup: Don't let your sluice overload. Clean up before the riffles become saturated with black sand.
  • Secondary recovery: Use a gold wheel or blue bowl for final cleanup of concentrates to capture the finest particles.
